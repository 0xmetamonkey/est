## The Pitch (30 seconds)

Your day is 6 films. Each film is 4 hours. You're the director.

The app helps you:
- Remember what scenes you want to film
- See time flow (instead of racing against it)
- Capture footage of your existence

It's NOT a productivity app. It's a life documentation tool.

---

## The Problem

**What people say:**
> "I want to do a lot of things, but..."
> - I forget what I wanted to do
> - I don't know what's happening  
> - I'm bored

**Current "solutions" suck:**
- Todo apps = Guilt when unchecked
- Time trackers = Stressful metrics
- Journals = Too much friction

---

## The Solution

**Shot by Shot** makes time visible and turns mundane life into filmable moments.

### How It Works
1. You have **12 "shots"** (activities): Coding, Yoga, Write, etc.
2. Your day is divided into **6x 4-hour "films"**
3. You choose which shots to "film" (aka do)
4. Timer shows **time flowing** (not running out)
5. You can **record actual footage** (video/audio/photos)
6. At the end, you have a **reel** of what you did

### Key Insight
When you're "filming" something, even mundane tasks become interesting. 

You're not "doing yoga." You're **filming a yoga scene**.  
You're not "just coding." You're **capturing footage of your work**.

Life becomes cinema.

## Target Users

**Primary:**
- **The Overwhelmed Multi-Hyphenate**
  - Wants to code AND paint AND exercise AND read
  - Forgets what they wanted to do
  - Feels like time disappears

**Secondary:**
- Creative professionals documenting process
- Self-improvement enthusiasts (without productivity pressure)
- People who journal but want less friction

**NOT for:**
- People who want traditional productivity tracking
- People who need team collaboration
- People who want cloud sync / social features
